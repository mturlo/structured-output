# Brief: Coffee subscription churn study

**Client:** Roastline (DTC coffee subscription, ~40k subscribers)
**Goal:** Understand why subscribers cancel after 2–3 deliveries.

## Background

Roastline ships fresh-roasted single-origin beans on a 2-week cadence. Retention drops sharply between months 1 and 3. Marketing assumes price; product suspects roast variety. We want quantified input from cancellers.

## Audience

Recently-churned subscribers (cancelled within last 60 days). Target n=400.

## Question sketch

Start with a screener confirming they were a Roastline subscriber and cancelled recently — single-choice yes/no.

Then ask them to pick the **single most important reason** they cancelled, from: price too high, didn't drink it fast enough, didn't like the roasts, prefer buying locally, switched to competitor, other. Use a single-choice question.

Follow up with a multi-select on **all other contributing factors** — same option list minus the one they already picked.

If they picked "didn't like the roasts," route them to a grid asking how they'd rate the last three shipments they received, on rows = shipment 1/2/3, columns = a 5-point scale (loved / liked / neutral / disliked / hated). Skip the grid for everyone else — route them past it to the next question.

Finally, an open-ended question: "What would have made you stay?" — max 500 chars.

Demographics (age band, region) are already in CRM, do not re-ask.
