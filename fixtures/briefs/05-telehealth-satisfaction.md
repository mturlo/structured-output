# Brief: Telehealth visit satisfaction

**Client:** Regional health system, telehealth product team
**Goal:** Post-visit satisfaction across video visits for primary care. Identify drop-off points in the patient journey.

## Audience

Patients who completed a video visit in the last 14 days. Sent via email 24h after visit. Target n=600.

## Question sketch

Confirm visit type first — single-choice: "What kind of visit did you have?" (primary care, urgent care, specialist, follow-up, not sure). Only "primary care" continues — terminate others, this wave is scoped.

Overall satisfaction: single-choice 5-point Likert ("very dissatisfied" → "very satisfied"). Required.

Journey grid: rows = "scheduling", "joining the video call", "talking with the doctor", "getting a prescription or follow-up plan". Columns = the same 5-point Likert as above.

Technical issues: multi-choice — "Did any of these happen during your visit?" Options: video froze, audio cut out, couldn't log in, doctor was late, doctor was rushed, none of these. "None of these" exclusive. Optional.

If they picked anything other than "none", route to a follow-up single-choice: "Did the issue affect the quality of your care?" (yes a lot / yes a little / no).

Recommendation: single-choice 0–10 NPS ("how likely are you to recommend our telehealth service to a friend or family member?"). Use 11 options 0..10.

Open-ended: "What's one thing we could do better?" — 400 char cap. Optional.

No demographics — pulled from EHR.
