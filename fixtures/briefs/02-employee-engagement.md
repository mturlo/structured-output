# Brief: Q3 employee engagement pulse

**Client:** Internal HR, mid-sized SaaS company (~1200 employees)
**Goal:** Quarterly pulse on engagement, manager quality, and remote-work satisfaction.

## Background

We run a long annual survey but want a shorter quarterly read on three themes: engagement, manager, and remote-work setup. Keep it under 5 minutes — fatigue is real.

## Audience

All employees. Anonymous. n unknown (whoever responds).

## Question sketch

Open with one engagement question — a single-choice 5-point Likert: "Overall, how engaged do you feel at work right now?" Options: very disengaged → very engaged.

Then a grid: rate your **manager** on these dimensions — rows = "gives clear direction", "gives useful feedback", "advocates for me", "removes blockers". Columns = strongly disagree / disagree / neutral / agree / strongly agree.

Multi-choice: which of these have you experienced in the past 90 days? Options: promotion, role change, new manager, team reorg, layoff in your team, none of the above. "None of the above" should be exclusive — if picked, clears the others.

If they picked "new manager", route them to a single-choice question on how the transition has been (smooth / mixed / rough). Skip otherwise.

Remote-work block: single-choice on current setup (fully remote / hybrid / fully in-office), then an open-ended "one thing that would improve your remote-work experience" — keep it short, 300 char cap.

End there. No demographics — anonymity matters.
