# Brief: Mayoral race poll, 8 weeks out

**Client:** Civic-data nonprofit
**Goal:** Snapshot of mayoral race in a mid-sized US city. Voter intent, top issues, incumbent approval.

## Audience

Registered voters in the city, screened on likelihood to vote. n=800.

## Question sketch

Screener: single-choice — "Are you registered to vote at your current address?" (yes / no / not sure). Terminate everyone except yes.

Likelihood: single-choice on intent to vote in November ("definitely", "probably", "50/50", "probably not", "definitely not"). Anyone below "probably" — route them straight to the demographics block at the end, skip the rest.

Vote intent: single-choice — "If the election were today, who would you vote for?" Options: Candidate A (incumbent), Candidate B, Candidate C, undecided, would not vote. Required.

Second choice (only asked if "undecided"): single-choice across A/B/C/still no preference. Route others past this question.

Top issues: multi-choice, "Which of these are your top concerns for the city?" Options: housing affordability, public safety, schools, transit, homelessness, taxes, other. Max 3 selections.

Issue rating grid: rows = housing, safety, schools, transit. Columns = how is the current mayor handling this — "very well", "somewhat well", "somewhat poorly", "very poorly", "no opinion".

Open-ended: "In one sentence, what's the most important issue facing the city?" — 200 char cap.

Demographics: age band, party affiliation (single-choice D/R/I/other/decline), neighborhood (single-choice from district list).
